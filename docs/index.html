Overview

Introduction

Phenotypic heterogeneity in cell populations has been shown to play critical roles in determining biologically and clinically significant phenotypes. The goal of this project is to identify loci in the human genomes that are associated with inter-individual variability in regulatory noise (robustness/variance QTLs), as measured by cell-to-cell variability in gene expression levels.

Data collection

We collected single cell RNA-seq data from YRI invididuals using the C1 platform. The 96 single cell samples of each C1 chip were pooled and sequenced on an Illumina HiSeq 2500.

Single cell sample identification were determined by examining the SNPs present in their RNA sequencing reads using the method verifyBamID. Using the identification of each single cell sample, we performed pairwise correlations between the samples and found that the effect of individual is stronger than the effect of C1 chip. We performed PCA on a subset of most variable genes and found that PC1 is most highly correlated with the percentage of detected genes and other metrics of sequencing depth. We also analyzed per-gene expression profiles and computed variance component estimates for the effects of individual and C1 batch.

Next, we performed quality control to identify low quality single cells. We filtered both over-expressed genes and lowly-expressed genes for downstream analysis.

QTL mapping

We looked at UMI count distribution stratified by genotype of the top 5 mean QTLs in YRI iPSCs. We investigated the concordance between single cell and bulk RNA-seq data and found that 50 cells are sufficient. With the attempt to estimate the power to detect mean and variance QTL, we took the strategy of resample scRNA-seq. We estimated means and dispersions per individual per gene and set up the infrastructure to call mean and variance QTLs.
